# Business Insights

## Executive KPI Snapshot

- Total sales: `217.0M`
- Total profit: `33.3M`
- Overall profit margin: `15.35%`
- Return rate: `4.55%`
- Average discount: `13.73%`
- Average delivery time: `3.59 days`
- Average customer rating: `4.03 / 5`

## Insight 1: Sales Trend

**Observation from dashboard**  
Sales grew from 2024 to 2025, but profitability did not improve at the same pace.

**Data evidence**  
2024 sales were `106.24M` with margin at `15.51%`.  
2025 sales increased to `110.78M`, but margin slipped to `15.20%`.

**Business interpretation**  
The business is still expanding, but the quality of growth weakened slightly. Higher discounting, weaker product mix, or rising return-related leakage may be offsetting topline gains.

**Recommended action or follow-up question**  
Review whether 2025 growth came disproportionately from lower-margin products, high-discount orders, or regions with weaker return outcomes.

## Insight 2: Regional Performance

**Observation from dashboard**  
South is the largest sales region, while East shows the highest return pressure.

**Data evidence**  
`South` delivered `64.69M` in sales, the highest among all regions.  
`East` recorded the highest regional margin at `15.55%`, but also the highest return rate at `4.91%`.  
`West` had the lowest regional margin at `15.14%`.

**Business interpretation**  
Regional performance is fairly balanced, but East may be masking profit leakage through returns, and West has the clearest room for margin improvement.

**Recommended action or follow-up question**  
Drill into East by state, category, and ship mode to identify what is driving return pressure, and test whether West’s lower margin is caused by discounting or category mix.

## Insight 3: Category Profitability

**Observation from dashboard**  
Technology is the main profit engine, while Furniture is the weakest major category.

**Data evidence**  
`Technology` generated `153.90M` in sales and `28.04M` in profit with an `18.22%` margin.  
`Furniture` generated `51.64M` in sales but only `3.56M` in profit with a `6.89%` margin.

**Business interpretation**  
Technology combines scale and margin, making it the strongest category for growth. Furniture brings meaningful revenue but destroys profitability relative to its size.

**Recommended action or follow-up question**  
Prioritize Technology for growth investment and evaluate whether Furniture needs pricing, sourcing, or assortment changes before more sales volume is pushed there.

## Insight 4: Sub-Category Profitability Risk

**Observation from dashboard**  
Furniture weakness is concentrated in specific sub-categories rather than spread evenly across the business.

**Data evidence**  
Low-margin sub-categories include:  
`Tables` at `5.67%` margin,  
`Bookcases` at `5.71%`,  
`Furnishings` at `7.86%`,  
`Chairs` at `8.25%`.

**Business interpretation**  
This suggests the Furniture problem is diagnosable and fixable. Leadership does not need to reduce the entire category uniformly if the main leakage comes from a few product groups.

**Recommended action or follow-up question**  
Run a focused review on Tables and Bookcases first: Are margins being compressed by discounts, product cost, freight, or unusually high returns?

## Insight 5: Customer Segment Behavior

**Observation from dashboard**  
Customer segments perform similarly, with no major structural weakness in one segment.

**Data evidence**  
`Home Office` margin is `15.51%`.  
`Consumer` margin is `15.34%`.  
`Corporate` margin is `15.18%`.

**Business interpretation**  
The business does not appear overexposed to one low-quality customer segment. Performance differences are more likely being driven by product mix, pricing strategy, and operational execution.

**Recommended action or follow-up question**  
Use segment filters mainly to refine opportunity targeting, not to identify the core business problem. Check whether segment differences become more meaningful only after filtering by category or region.

## Insight 6: Discount Impact

**Observation from dashboard**  
Profitability falls steadily as discount levels increase, and the deepest discounts become destructive.

**Data evidence**  
Profit margin declines from `20.56%` at `0.00` discount to `13.25%` at `0.20`, then to `8.55%` at `0.25`, `7.60%` at `0.30`, and `-4.95%` at `0.35`.

**Business interpretation**  
The current pricing approach likely overuses deep discounting in some situations. Sales generated at very high discount levels are not healthy growth if they erase profit.

**Recommended action or follow-up question**  
Introduce tighter approval controls for discounts above `25%` and test which categories or channels are most responsible for the `35%` loss-making orders.

## Insight 7: Shipping And Delivery Performance

**Observation from dashboard**  
Faster fulfillment is associated with lower return risk.

**Data evidence**  
`Same Day` shipping has a `2.49%` return rate and average delivery time of `0.40` days.  
`Standard Class` has a `4.60%` return rate and `4.71` average delivery days.  
`First Class` shows the highest return rate at `5.08%`.

**Business interpretation**  
Shipping experience appears to influence customer outcomes. Long delivery windows or service inconsistency may be increasing dissatisfaction, damage exposure, or mismatch between expectation and actual experience.

**Recommended action or follow-up question**  
Investigate whether First Class and Standard Class returns are tied to specific regions, products, or carriers, and assess whether delivery delays correlate with lower customer ratings.

## Insight 8: Return Pattern And Business Risk / Opportunity

**Observation from dashboard**  
Returns are concentrated in Furniture, creating a clear risk area and a potential profitability recovery opportunity.

**Data evidence**  
Category return rates are:  
`Furniture` at `7.67%`,  
`Office Supplies` at `3.65%`,  
`Technology` at `3.03%`.  
Overall return rate is `4.55%`.

**Business interpretation**  
Furniture is not only low margin but also the highest-return category, which compounds profit erosion. Fixing this one area could improve both customer experience and financial performance at the same time.

**Recommended action or follow-up question**  
Treat Furniture returns as a priority business risk. Review whether product quality, damage in transit, discount-led overselling, or incorrect customer expectations are driving the elevated return rate.

## Overall Business Opportunity

The strongest growth path is to scale what already works well:

- expand high-margin `Technology`
- control deep discounting
- reduce return-heavy Furniture leakage
- improve underperforming shipping outcomes

If leadership improves those four levers together, the company can likely grow sales while also improving margin quality rather than sacrificing profit for volume.
